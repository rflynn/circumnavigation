Over his long career Fonseca inevitably made many enemies, most notably Bartolomé de las Casas, known as the Defender of the Indians, who denounced him for his indifference to the cruelties that Spanish settlers inflicted on the native population of the new lands. He also clashed with Hernan Cortés, conqueror of Mexico, which led to his removal from office in 1523, the year before his death.
